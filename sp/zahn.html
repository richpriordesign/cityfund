Zahn Innovation Center

Enables entrepreneurial and social innovation goals for CCNY students.

Support

The City College Fund supports CCNY in its contributions to the cultural, social and economic life of New York. Recently, in response to the need to diversify and expand New York's economy, the Fund helped the College to establish the Zahn Innovation Center through grants from alumnus Irwin Zahn '48 and the Moxie Foundation, and Manhattan Borough President Scott Stringer.

It is NYC's first hardware start-up offering space, equipment, seed funding, and expert technical and entrepreneurship advice. It is NYC's first hardware start-up offering space, equipment, seed funding, and expert technical and entrepreneurship advice to CCNY students, faculty, alumni and community members seeking to develop their own companies. The Center also funds more than $100,000 in prize money each year for the Zahn Entrepreneurship Prize, The Kaylie Hardware Prize, and The Zahn Social Innovation Prize.

Through its services, the Zahn Center will enable CCNY's students to pursue their entrepreneurial and social innovation goals while they are still in college, a truly remarkable opportunity. These students will be better prepared and more likely to start their own business or serve as valuable employees for startups, particularly in New York.

About

The City College Fund is a tax-exempt 501c(3) non-profit fundraising organization, established in 1946 to support the City College of New York in its mission to provide access and excellence in higher education, particularly to students who might not otherwise be able to afford to go to college. Gifts to The City College Fund directly benefit City College's extraordinary students,renowned faculty, innovative programs and schools.
